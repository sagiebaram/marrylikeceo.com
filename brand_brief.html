09 · Brand Guardrails
Do This. Not That.
✓ Do
Speak to the reader’s intelligence, not their wounds
Name the system explicitly — with steps, frameworks, and names
Use Ariel’s story as evidence, not martyrdom
Reference Israeli / Jewish cultural nuance with lightness and confidence
Anchor every content piece to a concrete, actionable output
Sound like the smartest, most grounded friend at the table
Use the real logos — light on light surfaces, dark on dark surfaces
✗ Don’t
Use generic phrases like “you deserve love” or “trust the process”
Frame men as adversaries, obstacles, or the enemy
Position the reader as broken, lost, or a victim of their own story
Over-use pink, floating hearts, or bridal stock imagery
Mix light and dark palette colours — always use the full set for the context
Lead with emotion — lead with insight, then follow with emotion
Use more than one purple or crimson accent per composition
